Chapter 3.1

The time is.. eleven fifty-one…

What do I even do to burn time..?

It’d be better if I could leave with Hayashi right away..

…

One thirty-three…

Why is my watch ticking so slow..?

..And here I thought he’d be excited… but he’s just sleeping..

Should I just sleep too…?

…

Four forty-nine.. sleeping really does help… but can’t this go any faster?

..Hayashi on the other hand was already awake… Though I kept my head buried in my arms, I turned to look at him—he’s just..

“Look at the board, not me.”

“...” I didn’t utter a response, but I didn’t look at the board either.

“You’re that excited?”

“Me?” I asked, and he just stared at me. “..Why would I be?”

I scratched my head in annoyance, asking such stupid questions..

…

Then finally… classes came to a halt. I glanced at Hayashi, watching if he’d stand and leave right away. He noticed me eyeing him, and once he stood—I immediately followed suit; I could almost bolt out the school in excitement.

After we tediously swapped our indoor shoes with the outside pair—stepping outside… it felt like the air got chiller.

It’s exciting.. winter.

“Isn’t it exciting?” he asked.

“..What does?”

“Winter.”

“...You—read my mind.”

“Oh, you were thinking the same thing?”

“..Do you like it?” I asked.

“I do.” he said as I stared at the pale sky. “During winter, it just feels like the world’s slower. I can take my time and just relax.”

Ngh… ever since we stepped out, my head’s been hurting; everything feels fragmented..

“Yeah, I agree—it just feels ethereal.” I slowly responded.

I can’t hear Hayashi properly.

This feeling… it’s almost like when..

“Aihara-san…” this unpleasant voice ringing in my head… Sakuragi.. “What’s this supposed to be?” he muttered, tone ringing with mockery. I suddenly felt myself being pulled towards him—the headache once mild… began to feel like needles being driven deeper down my brain..

“What do you think you’re doing?” a sudden voice of clarity—setting a halt to the torment in my head… Hayashi..?

His voice.. different.

He brushed Sakuragi aside as if swatting away a leaf.

Gently—he grabbed my wrist and pulled me away from Sakuragi.

“Hayashi..” I called.

When I finally noticed, I was behind him. The headache weakened—my vision clearer, and the constant ringing noise in my head somewhat subsided.. though it’s still there.

Why are they… just staring at each other..?

“Here I thought you sounded familiar…” Sakuragi laughed, piercing my ears. “What’s our soccer genius doing here?”

..Soccer genius..?

“I thought I made it clear to everyone that Aihara’s mine?” Sakuragi claimed, yet I couldn’t respond back.

I may have felt strange… my head may have been buzzing with pain—but there was something far more different.

..Hayashi.

“What’s with this wannabe protagonist of a third-rate sports manga doing here?” he retaliated with a cold voice. “I thought I was done with your petty tantrums.”

I reflexively took a step back because all of a sudden.. my headache worsened as Sakuragi’s fist flew towards Hayashi—but it never connected.

Hayashi had already caught it with surgical precision, his posture remained perfectly still… he didn’t even move his feet..

“Aihara… to think you had to tolerate this clown.”

“What’d you call me?!”

“A bottom-feeder blaming his mediocrity on me… two times now.”

Instantly after speaking, Hayashi moved again.. efficiently.

He immediately pinned Sakuragi to the pavement beneath his knee..

So… effortless..

“For too long I’ve held back from your antics. I won’t refrain from destroying your career if you pull off a stunt like this again.” Hayashi stated.

..Did they have… history..?

…For a moment I stood there, unaware of the words being exchanged as the conflict finally settled. From the constant buzz that rang my head earlier, the world was suddenly too silent—I couldn’t move.

I felt… disconnected.

I couldn’t move a muscle.

Until someone held my arms that I snapped back into place.

I could hear again.

I could see again.

Hayashi—holding my arms, having shaken me.

“What happened? Are you alright..?” he asked, his hand leaving my arm and placed upon my forehead, pressing against my neck.

“..I’m fine…” I muttered. “What happened?”

“I made him leave, I already told him off.”

“I see..”

“You’re disoriented aren’t you..?” he asked, so spot on…

Do I tell him..?

“Hold onto me—I’ll escort you to your dorm.” he extended his arm towards me..

“No..” I shook my head. “We’re going on a date..”

“..You sure?”

“..Yeah.” Though it took some time to convince him that I was fine, he finally began to lead the way. “...May I?”

He looked at me wondering what I was asking for.

I extended my hand, reaching towards him.

There I held onto his wrist.

For.. stability.
